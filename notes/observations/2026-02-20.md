# Observations — 2026-02-20

## Binding vs. Decomposition — Two Threads, One Argument (CLOSED)

**Source:** Obsidian research notes (pre-existing) + experimental results same session

---

### Thread 1: Binding (attention pattern analysis)
- Does "reader" specifically attend to "screen" more than other tokens?
- Pythia 2.8B: Yes — specific binding heads identified (L5H23, L6H13, L10H19, etc.)
- GPT-2 Medium/Large: No — salience pattern (all tokens attend to "screen")
- GPT-2 XL: No — diffuse/noise pattern. Largest single head contribution across
  all 48 layers: L31H11 at 1.95 logits, L34H22 at 1.19. No head cluster
  remotely resembling Pythia binding heads.
- **Finding:** Binding is architecture-specific. Dung's EB* metric, built on
  Pythia binding observations, does not generalize to GPT-2.

### Thread 2: Where does knowledge live? (decomposition)

#### Prompt A: "A blind person uses a screen" → target: ` reader`
Retrieval-from-cue task. Model must complete the compound from partial input.

**GPT-2 Small (fails — input fixation):**
- MLP dominant throughout, peaks at L11: 33 logits
- Logit lens: " screen" dominates every layer — model echoes its own input
- Distinct failure mode: not syntactic prior, not knowledge absence — input fixation

**GPT-2 Small "A screen reader is" (fails — grammatical completion):**
- Attention dominant early (L6: 9.5), large MLP late (L10: 20.8, L11: 20.8)
- Logit lens: " not" for most layers, drifts to " a" — grammatically correct, semantically empty
- Model knows the grammar, not the concept. Graph is essentially flat.

**GPT-2 Medium (fails — syntactic override):**
- MLP dominant from layer 0 — L20: 35.3 logits, L22: **36.3 logits** for " reader"
- Logit lens: " to" wins at layer 23, " reader" at rank 2
- MLP is screaming the right answer. Syntactic prior ("uses a screen to [verb]") 
  overrides 36 logits of factual signal.
- Direct evidence that MLP storage and output retrieval are dissociable.

**GPT-2 XL (succeeds — MLP retrieval):**
- MLP dominant throughout. Late-layer surge: L40: 9.1, L41: 8.7, L43: **12.8**, L44: **11.6** logits
- Attention never exceeds 4 logits in late layers
- Logit lens: semantic wandering — "writers" → "cap" → "camera" → "capture" → "protector"
  then " reader" hits rank 1 at layer 43, holds through L47 at 26.4%
- No binding heads. Pure MLP retrieval.

#### Pythia Scale Ladder: "A blind person uses a screen" → ` reader`

**Pythia 160M (fails — MLP strong, syntactic override):**
- MLP peaks at L11: **35.4 logits**. Attention at same layer: 6.98
- Logit lens: " to" at rank 29, 0.16% probability
- Multiple heads mobilize at L11 simultaneously (L11H0: 1.24, L11H2: 1.39, L11H11: 1.10)
- Everything jammed into final layer. No binding.

**Pythia 410M (fails — knowledge not encoded):**
- MLP barely fires, max ~2.3 logits at L19. Qualitatively different from 160M.
- Logit lens: "er" dominates L8-L16 — model trying to complete a word, not retrieve a concept
- Flat head contributions throughout. No binding.
- Most interesting failure: 410M doesn't even have the knowledge stored, unlike 160M.

**Pythia 1B (partial — anomalous architecture):**
- L14H2: **15.97 logits** — dominant binding head
- L14 total: ATTN 18.77 vs MLP 5.63
- Logit lens: " Web" hits rank 1 at L15 with 37% — correct domain, wrong token
- **IMPORTANT:** 1B has different architecture — parallel attention+MLP, expanded MLP (8192)
- In parallel architecture, attention and MLP read same residual stream simultaneously
- L14H2 is doing direct retrieval, not routing to MLP
- Not directly comparable to other models on the ladder

**Pythia 2.8B (succeeds — MLP dominant with late reversal):**
- MLP dominant from L17 onward, peaks L21: **3.64 logits**
- Logit lens semantic journey: noise → "POSE" → " blind" (L17-L19, rank 10-63) 
  → "-" (L20-L29, hyphenated compound territory) → " reader" rank 3 at L30 →
  **" reader" rank 1 at L31, 46.7%**
- Model reasons: blind person → assistive device → hyphenated compound → screen reader
- **L31 MLP: -3.54 logits** — final layer MLP actively suppresses " reader"
  while accumulated residual stream momentum wins anyway
- In parallel architecture: MLP at L31 making independent judgment, voting against,
  getting overruled by 14 layers of positive signal
- No binding heads. Max single head: L18H13 at 1.25, L21H15 at 1.29 — noise.

#### Prompt B: "A screen reader is" → target: ` software`
Definition task. "Screen reader" is the subject, model generates description.
This matches the syntactic frame of prior binding work.

**Pythia 2.8B (definition prompt):**
- Attention dominant L10-L21 — L16: **5.09 ATTN vs 0.62 MLP**
- Specific heads firing: L16H1: 1.65, L16H2: 1.04, L16H21: 1.19, L21H6: 2.08, L27H9: 1.79
- These are the binding heads — they appear on definition prompts, not retrieval prompts
- Logit lens: drifts through "usually/always/a" territory, " a" at rank 3, 3.3% at L31
- Binding heads fire but produce weaker behavioral output than MLP retrieval

---

### Critical Finding: Binding is prompt-type specific, not concept-specific

Same model (Pythia 2.8B), same concept ("screen reader"), completely different
computational strategy depending on prompt structure:

| Prompt type | Mechanism | Binding heads | Behavioral success |
|-------------|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Retrieval-from-cue | MLP dominant | No | 46.7% ✓ |
| Definition frame | ATTN dominant | Yes — L16, L21, L27 | 3.3% ✗ |

Binding appears when "screen reader" is the **subject** being elaborated.
MLP retrieval appears when "screen reader" is the **target** to be retrieved.
These are different cognitive operations requiring different mechanisms.

### Implications for paper 3 argument

1. Binding is prompt-type specific within the same model and concept
2. Binding appears in definition/elaboration contexts, not retrieval contexts
3. MLP retrieval is the mechanism for successful compound term completion
4. Prior binding work (EB*) used exclusively definition-frame prompts —
   measuring binding-as-consolidation, not binding-as-retrieval
5. The retrieval-from-cue regime, more ecologically valid for real accessibility tasks,
   is MLP dominated with no binding across all models tested
6. Encoding and retrieval are dissociable: 160M and Medium have strong MLP signal
   but fail behaviorally — prime candidates for probing (Paper 2)

---

### Probing implications (Paper 2)

GPT-2 Small's flat logit lens despite building MLP signal is the cleanest 
encoding/retrieval dissociation in the dataset. If probe shows high accuracy
at L9-L11 on GPT-2 Small despite near-zero output probability, that's the
paper 2 argument in its purest form: the knowledge is geometrically encoded,
the output mechanism cannot find it.
